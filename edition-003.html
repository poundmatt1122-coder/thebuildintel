This Week

California lost 17% of its refining capacity. Diesel is $6.94/gallon. Asphalt binder hit $565/ton. Every paving bid in the western US written more than 30 days ago is underpriced.

The closures of Phillips 66 Wilmington and Valero Benicia have removed roughly 290,000 barrels per day of refining capacity from California — and the market is responding. The $1.73/gallon premium California contractors are now paying over the national average is structural, not temporary. No new refining capacity is coming online. The binder market is following crude oil higher. Every paving contractor in CA, AZ, and NV needs to revisit their open bids and add escalation clauses to their active contracts before the next price move.

"The data center pipeline is enormous — $130B in the West alone — but community opposition is the variable that will determine who wins the work. Contractors building public engagement capability today are building a moat."

Aggregates are rising in every urban western market driven by zoning constraints limiting new quarry permits and fuel costs compounding transport prices. The 30-60 day outlook across aggregates, ready-mix, and cement is Rising. The labor market is not loosening — 349,000 net new workers still needed in 2026 and wages climbing 6-8% in western markets.
