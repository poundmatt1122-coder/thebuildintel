Executive Summary

California lost 17% of its refining capacity with the closures of Phillips 66 Wilmington and Valero Benicia — and the numbers are showing up at the pump, with California diesel now at $6.94/gallon versus a $5.21 national average, a $1.73/gallon premium that hits every contractor operating west of the Rockies. Asphalt oil binder hit $565/ton in June, up from $557 in May and trending higher tied directly to crude oil and refinery capacity — every paving bid in CA, AZ, and NV is exposed. Aggregates are rising in urban western markets with crushed stone running $25–45/ton and constrained supply from zoning and land restrictions compounding fuel-driven transport costs. The data center pipeline remains the single largest demand driver for construction materials in the West but $130B in projects remain stalled — the contractors building community engagement capability now will win the work when opposition is resolved.
