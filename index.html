This Week

California lost 17% of its refining capacity. Diesel is $6.94/gallon. Asphalt binder hit $565/ton. Every paving bid in the western US written more than 30 days ago is likely underpriced.

The closures of Phillips 66 Wilmington and Valero Benicia have removed roughly 290,000 barrels per day of California refining capacity — and the market is responding. The $1.73/gallon premium California contractors are now paying over the national average is structural, not temporary. No new refining capacity is coming online. The binder market is following crude oil higher. Every paving contractor in CA, AZ, and NV needs to revisit open bids and add escalation clauses to active contracts before the next price move.

"The data center pipeline is enormous — $130B in the West alone — but community opposition is the variable that determines who wins the work. Contractors building public engagement capability today are building a competitive moat."

Aggregates are rising in every urban western market, driven by zoning constraints limiting new quarry permits and fuel costs compounding transport prices. The 30-60 day outlook across aggregates, ready-mix, and cement is Rising. Labor is not loosening — 349,000 net new workers still needed in 2026 and wages climbing 6-8% in western markets. The water situation in Arizona, Nevada, and Colorado is becoming a go/no-go factor for large projects — not just an environmental consideration.

West Region Spotlight

Southern California Priority sub-region

Caltrans SoCal letting active — weekly bid packages in the tens of millions · Port of Long Beach cement imports — critical supply watch

The LA Basin carries the most acute refinery exposure of any western sub-market — both Phillips 66 Wilmington and Valero Benicia were local operations whose closure is felt directly in SoCal fuel and binder pricing. Caltrans bid results active weekly with infrastructure packages across the region. Data center development facing opposition in multiple LA Basin jurisdictions. Inland Empire logistics and industrial construction pipeline remains strong driven by e-commerce and nearshoring demand. San Diego military and infrastructure projects providing steady public sector pipeline. Wildfire rebuilding ongoing in LA and surrounding counties adding residential demand. Port of Long Beach cement import volumes are the most important supply signal for SoCal ready-mix pricing — any port disruption tightens the market within weeks.
