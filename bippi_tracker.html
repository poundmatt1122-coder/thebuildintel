What is the Build Intel Price Pressure Index?

The BIPPI measures real-world cost inflation as experienced by western US construction producers — weighted by actual input cost composition, not a government basket of consumer goods. Unlike CPI, which uses hedonic quality adjustments, substitution bias, and owner's equivalent rent to measure consumer inflation, BIPPI tracks what you actually pay for the inputs that drive your cost structure every week. Two sector-specific indexes are published: BIPPI-A for asphalt producers and paving contractors, and BIPPI-C for ready-mix concrete producers. Base period March 2026 = 100. A reading of 104 means your input costs are 4% higher than March 2026.

What the gap means: The federal government's CPI says prices rose 2.1% over the last three months. For an asphalt producer in California, the real number is 6.8% — 3.2 times faster. For a ready-mix concrete producer, it's 4.2% — twice as fast. This gap exists because CPI tracks consumer goods and uses methodological adjustments (hedonic quality adjustments, substitution bias, owner's equivalent rent) that systematically understate industrial input cost inflation. The BIPPI tracks what you actually pay — no adjustments, no substitutions, just the real numbers from the sources your business depends on.

What This Means for Your Business

If you built your Q2 2026 budget using CPI as your inflation assumption, you underestimated your cost structure by 2-5 percentage points depending on your sector. An asphalt producer who budgeted for 2% cost inflation is actually running at 6.8% — that's a gap of nearly 5 points that comes directly out of margin. For a ready-mix producer, the gap is roughly 2 points. The California refinery crisis is the primary driver of the BIPPI-A divergence — diesel at $6.94/gallon contributes 3.35 index points out of the total 6.8 point move. If diesel normalizes as refinery capacity is restored, BIPPI-A will moderate. But with no new capacity coming online in 2026, the base case is continued elevated diesel and binder costs through H2 2026.

Data Sources — BIPPI Methodology

Asphalt oil binder: Caltrans Statewide Crude Oil Price Index · March 2026 base estimated from Caltrans monthly data trend

Diesel fuel (California): EIA Weekly Retail Diesel Prices · California series EMD_EPD2D_PTE_SCA_DPG

Aggregates (crushed stone / sand and gravel): USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026 · Western US regional estimates

Construction labor wages: BLS CES2000000003 via FRED · Average hourly earnings all employees construction

Cement pricing: USGS Cement Commodity Summary 2026 · Argus Media US Cement Market 2026

CPI Urban comparison: BLS Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U)

Hot mix asphalt pricing reference: AsphaPro HMA Price Guide April 2026

Ready-mix concrete pricing reference: Concrete Network 2026 Pricing Guide · Constructem April 2026
