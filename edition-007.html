My hope is that it helps with the messaging that I am sure all of us have to put together for our businesses — whether that is talking to customers, leadership teams, or planning for the back half of the year. The pipeline is there. It is just a matter of when these projects move and how they impact your specific market.

As always — if you are seeing something different in your market or have a project you think we should be tracking, reach out. That feedback makes this better for everyone.

Western US Large Project Pipeline — July 2026

The headline market sentiment may be flat to slightly down across the western US right now. But underneath the weekly noise is a project pipeline that is genuinely historic in scale. The projects listed below are publicly announced, publicly sourced, and actively moving. They represent hundreds of billions of dollars in construction demand across all five western regions. Not all of it hits the market at once — but knowing where it is and when it moves is exactly what this report is built to track.

Semiconductor and Advanced Manufacturing $57B+ tracked
Desert SW

TSMC Arizona — GigaFab Campus

North Phoenix, Maricopa County, Arizona

The largest foreign direct investment in US manufacturing history. Fab 1 in production. Fab 2 equipment installation accelerating into Q3 2026 with 3nm production now targeted 2027 ahead of prior 2028 schedule. Fab 3 under construction. Fab 4 permits advancing. The Phoenix construction market is absorbing a demand surge with no parallel in the western US — concrete, aggregates, structural steel, and specialized cleanroom trades all competing simultaneously across multiple active fab phases.

Mountain West

Micron Technology — Boise Memory Fab Campus

Southeast Boise, Idaho

The first new domestic memory fab in two decades. Fab 1 (ID1) shell complete with cleanroom fit-out the dominant work scope through the back half of 2026. Fab 2 (ID2) construction beginning 2026 with operations targeted end of 2028. The cast-in-place concrete program is approximately 220,000 cubic yards. Steelwork totals roughly 28,000 tons. At peak the project carries approximately 6,000 trades workers on site under the Yates/Layton joint venture. CHIPS Act grants of $6.1 billion support the Idaho program. Additional phases beyond Fab 2 are planned as part of Micron's broader $200B US expansion.

SoCal

Prado Dam Spillway Modification & Whittier Narrows Dam Safety

Santa Ana River near Corona (Prado) and Los Angeles County (Whittier Narrows), California

US Army Corps of Engineers awarded $1.86 billion in contracts to Barnard Construction on March 23, 2026. The $1.13 billion Prado Dam Spillway Modification project will improve flood protection for 1.4 million people and $200 billion in infrastructure. The concurrent $735 million Whittier Narrows contract addresses the most urban dam in the Army Corps western portfolio. Major construction is slated to begin spring 2027 with completion by 2033. Delivered via integrated design-and-construction with early contractor involvement.

California, Idaho, Utah, Wyoming (One Big Beautiful Bill Act)

Department of Interior announced $889 million in investments for critical water infrastructure across six western states in March 2026. California receives $540 million — the largest share — targeting Central Valley Project infrastructure: Delta-Mendota Canal $235 million (embankment upgrades and potential new concrete-lined segment), Friant-Kern Canal $200 million (subsidence bottleneck repairs), San Luis Canal $50 million. Policy Watch — Two Deadlines Still Approaching

The project pipeline above represents hundreds of billions of dollars in real western US construction demand. But two policy deadlines are sitting on top of it that could slow or accelerate how quickly that demand reaches contractors and suppliers. Both deserve your attention before year-end.

IIJA — 80 days

Congress heading to recess with no Senate markup

The BUILD America 250 Act passed the House T&I Committee 62-2 on May 22 and has seen no new action since. The Senate has no markup scheduled. House recess begins July 23. Senate recess begins July 31. That leaves roughly 10 legislative days before recess and minimal time after return before the September 30 deadline. A Continuing Resolution is increasingly the base case. Under a CR, existing projects continue but state DOTs hesitate to let large new multi-year contracts. House T&I ↗ AASHTO ↗

Colorado River — 172 days

Final EIS not yet published — decision required before October 1

The Bureau of Reclamation has not yet published the Final Environmental Impact Statement for Post-2026 Colorado River Operations as of July 12. A decision is legally required before October 1 — the start of the 2027 water year. Lake Powell is at 3,524 feet and Lake Mead is heading toward 1,040 feet. Any contractor bidding large projects in Phoenix, Las Vegas, or Tucson for 2027 delivery must verify water availability before pricing. USBR ↗ ADWR ↗

Bottom line on policy: The pipeline is real and it is large. The IIJA and Colorado River situations create uncertainty around timing — not around the existence of the work. Contractors who use this window to lock crews, secure material commitments, and position for the projects above will be better positioned than those waiting for policy certainty before acting. The western US construction market feels flat to slightly down in day-to-day sentiment — but the underlying project pipeline tells a different story. The projects tracked in this edition alone represent over $125 billion in total investment across all five western regions. Semiconductor fabs in Arizona and Idaho, water infrastructure in California and Wyoming, transit in the Bay Area and Seattle, high-speed rail between Las Vegas and LA, and energy transmission across New Mexico and Arizona.
