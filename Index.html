Executive Summary

Construction input prices surged 2.6% in May alone — now nearly 10% above year-ago levels — driven by steel tariffs and energy costs with no near-term relief in sight. Labor shortages remain structurally broken: the industry needs 349,000 net new workers in 2026 and wages are rising 4-10%+ across firms, hitting western markets hardest. The data center pipeline is enormous ($130B+) but community opposition is blocking or delaying 75+ projects — contractors with public engagement experience have a real edge. Regulatory tailwinds are emerging via EPA and Army Corps streamlining, but the 30-60 day bid environment looks squeezed on all fronts.

Up to 50% tariffs on imports sustaining elevated prices. Mill products and fabricated metals saw significant YoY increases with no pullback forecast through H2 2026.

Demand Signal - Labor Market

Wages rising fast High impact Construction Dive

Average hourly earnings in construction running $38+. Many firms reporting 4-10%+ wage increases YoY amid acute competition for skilled trades. Western markets (CA, AZ) among the most affected.

349,000 net new workers needed in 2026 Critical ABC via Yahoo Finance

The industry must attract 349,000 net new workers this year - rising to 456,000 in 2027. Shortages sharpest in specialized trades and data center construction work.

Immigration enforcement adding pressure in CA and AZ

Workforce disruptions being reported in California tied to immigration enforcement, compounding existing skilled trade gaps. AZ also seeing wage-driven competition.

Takeaway: Build labor cost contingency into every bid - 5-8% minimum in CA and AZ. Lock crew commitments before award on large projects; the open market will only get tighter.

Week in Review - Top 3 Takeaways

1

Reprice your bids now. A 2.6% single-month input cost surge - with materials up ~10% year-over-year - means any bid priced on data more than 45-60 days old is likely underpriced. Steel and diesel are the sharpest exposures. Add escalation buffers and push for material price adjustment clauses on longer-duration contracts.

ABC Materials Report
2

Crew availability is your real schedule risk. With 349,000 workers still needed nationally and wages climbing 4-10%+, the labor market is not loosening. In CA and AZ add workforce contingency to every bid, secure crew commitments early, and expect delays if relying on the open market for specialized trades.

ABC Workforce Report
3

The data center gold rush has a community problem. $130B in projects blocked by local opposition is a structural shift - not a blip. Contractors pursuing this sector need to factor public acceptance risk into go/no-go decisions. EPA and Army Corps reforms are creating genuine permitting speed advantages for projects that do get local buy-in.
